Frequently Asked Questions

What is BuddyBlink? expand_more
BuddyBlink is a check-in app. You set up a schedule (e.g., check in once a day), and if you miss a check-in, BuddyBlink automatically notifies your designated contacts via Telegram or email.
How do I check in? expand_more
When it's time to check in, you'll receive a push notification on your phone. Simply tap the "I'm Okay" button in the app to acknowledge your check-in. That's it — one tap.
What happens if I miss a check-in? expand_more
If you miss the number of check-ins you've configured (e.g., 1 or 2 missed), BuddyBlink automatically sends your message to the contacts assigned to that schedule, through the channels you've set up (Telegram or email).
What's included in the free plan? expand_more
The free plan includes up to 3 check-in schedules, up to 3 contacts, check-in intervals from every 2 hours, Telegram and email notifications (up to 10 emails per month), and custom safety messages. No credit card required.
What does Premium include? expand_more
Premium unlocks up to 50 check-in schedules, 50 contacts, check-in intervals from every 1 minute, Telegram & email notifications, and unlimited email notifications.
How do I connect a contact via Telegram? expand_more
After adding a contact, tap "Link Telegram" next to that contact. A unique link will be generated — send it to your contact. When they open it and start the BuddyBlink Telegram bot, their Telegram account is automatically linked. Links expire after 72 hours for security.
How do I delete my account? expand_more
Go to Settings > Delete Account in the app. This permanently deletes all your data — including contacts, schedules, check-in history, and notification logs — within 30 days. This action cannot be undone.
Is BuddyBlink available on iOS? expand_more
BuddyBlink is currently available on Android via Google Play. iOS support is coming soon — stay tuned for updates.
Is BuddyBlink an emergency service? expand_more
No. BuddyBlink is a best-effort notification tool — not a guaranteed emergency response service. It depends on internet connectivity, third-party services (Telegram, email providers), and your device being operational. In a life-threatening emergency, always contact emergency services (911, 112, etc.) directly.
